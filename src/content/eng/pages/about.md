## Current Roles

I currently work as a **researcher** and **chief data officer** at the National Institute of Forensic Science and Criminology ([NICC](/eng/nicc/)), in Belgium. 

My recent activity focuses on the integration of criminal justice data through knowledge graphs and the implementation of a data and artificial intelligence governance strategy.

I am also a **research collaborator** at [UCLouvain](/eng/uclouvain/). 

## Career Path

My professional path has led me to work in Belgium, the United States, Canada, and Italy.

I trained in **cognitive psychology** and received additional training in **statistics** at [UCLouvain](/eng/uclouvain/), then additional training in **artificial intelligence** at [KU Leuven](/eng/kuleuven/). 

I then obtained a **PhD in psycholinguistics** at the [University of Memphis](/eng/uofm/). 

Finally, I completed two postdoctorates, one in cognitive psychology at [Laval University](/eng/ulaval/) and the other in computational linguistics at the [University of Pisa](/eng/unipi/).


## Fields of Work

My work today is at the intersection of several fields:

- **criminal justice data** (forensic DNA intelligence, recidivism, criminal careers, etc.);
- **knowledge graphs** and graph-oriented databases;
- **data governance** and reasoned use of AI in public institutions.

## Methods and Tools

I work with experimental, statistical, and computational methods. On the technical side, I mainly use **Linux**, **Bash**, **Python**, **R**, **SQL**, **Neo4j**, **Jupyter** and **LaTeX**. I place particular importance on data quality, process traceability, and clear documentation.

## Benchmarks

Over the years, I have worked on language acquisition, computational linguistics, distributional semantic models, criminal databases, radicalization, recidivism, and knowledge graphs. I have also taught at the university and contributed to the preparation or management of several funded projects.
