Ubi bene ibi patria

This Latin motto litteraly means Where (you are) well, there (is your) fatherland.

It is similar to a variety of expressions, some of which are discussed by Erasmus of Rotterdam in his comment of adage ¶ 1193:

Quaevis terra patria (To have the world as fatherland/motherland).

Ellic enim patria est, ubi tibi sit bene (Where you are well, there really is your fatherland).

Patria est, ubicumque est bene (Fatherland is where you feel good).

Solum omne patria, prospere quicumque agit (Whoever has a prosperous life, has any land as fatherland).
