I obtained a Doctoral degree in psycholinguistics at the University of Memphis, Memphis, Tennessee, under the supervision of Prof. Dr Max M. Louwerse. My PhD dissertation is entitled The role of feedback in learning form-meaning mappings.

I participated to research involving embodied theories of cognition, multimodal conversations, conversational agents, eye-tracking, computational modeling and artificial language acquisition, working at the Institute for Intelligent Systems (IIS), in the FedEx Institute of Technology (FIT).

I also taught four undergraduate courses during two semesters in the psychology department: (1) Introduction to psychological research and (2) Thinking and cognitive processes.
